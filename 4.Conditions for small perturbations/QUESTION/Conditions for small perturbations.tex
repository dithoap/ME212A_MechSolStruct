\title{\textbf{Conditions for small perturbations}}
\date{}

\maketitle

\vspace{-1cm}


We consider the transformation  $\T{x}=\T{\Phi}(\T{X},t)$ defined by: 
\begin{eqnarray}
x_1 & = & X_1+X_2 \\ 
x_2 & = & X_2 \\ 
x_3 & = & X_3 
\end{eqnarray}

\begin{description}
\item \textbf{Question:} Make a graphical representation of the reference configuration and of the deformed configuration. Calculate the gradient of the transformation, $\TT{F}$.
\item \textbf{Question:} Is this an homogenous transformation? It is acceptable from a physical point of view?
\item \textbf{Question:} Calculate the expansion of the following vectors: $\T{e}_1$, $\T{e}_2$ and $\frac{\T{e}_1+\T{e}_2}{\sqrt{2}}$
\item \textbf{Question:} Calculate $\TT{e}$ and $\TT{\varepsilon}$? Can we consider we are in small perturbations?
\item \textbf{Question:} Consider the transformation $x_1=X_1+\alpha \cdot X_2$, $x_2=X_2$ and $x_3=X_3$. What is the condition for having small perturbations?
\end{description}
